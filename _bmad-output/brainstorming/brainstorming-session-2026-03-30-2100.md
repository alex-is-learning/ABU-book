### Session Setup

Alex is writing a book about the A/B/U framework — an epistemological model where:
- **Bs** (green) = consolidated axioms/core beliefs, clustered centrally
- **As** (red) = active thoughts/ideas, radiating outward in layers
- **Us** (purple) = external inputs that either get absorbed or forgotten

Key "events" to visualize: A frontier growth, A→B maturation, U approach/filtering. Output as web-renderable GIFs showing each event. 3D, brain-like aesthetic using Three.js and force-directed graphs.

## Technique Execution Results

### Analogical Thinking

**[Analogy #1]**: Cell Biology
*Concept*: The B-core as nucleus, As as cytoplasmic organelles radiating outward, Us as molecules approaching membrane receptors — some binding and entering, others bouncing off and diffusing away.
*Novelty*: Gives us the "selective permeability" visual — Us don't just appear, they *dock* at receptor sites. Rejection isn't harsh, it's just a failure to bind.

**[Analogy #2]**: Receptor Binding Affinity
*Concept*: External inputs approach and attempt to dock. Strong agonists snap into place with a satisfying click — immediate colour change to red (A), locked in. Weak agonists wobble loosely in the receptor, flicker purple, and drift away. The visual speed and firmness of the binding tells the viewer everything.
*Novelty*: The audience doesn't need a label to understand what's happening — the animation's physicality communicates strong vs weak fit intuitively.

**[Analogy #3]**: Social Circle / Trust Layers
*Concept*: Bs are your inner circle — trusted, close, foundational relationships. As are acquaintances at various distances. Us are strangers — some click and become acquaintances (As), others you forget by morning.
*Novelty*: Everyone understands social trust intuitively. The spatial metaphor of "inner circle" maps perfectly to the 3D layout.

**[Analogy #4]**: Crystallization / Solidification
*Concept*: The B-core is a growing crystal. As are molecules in solution. When conditions are right, an A locks into the crystal lattice — it solidifies. Each new B changes the shape of what can attach next.
*Novelty*: Captures the idea that Bs aren't just stored beliefs — they're structural.

**[Analogy #5]**: Concept-Shaped Holes / Negative Space
*Concept*: Before an A matures into a B, there's an invisible gap in the B-core where it should be. The moment of B-crystallization is also the moment you realize the hole was always there. Example: "I am liked" taking years despite evidence being there all along.
*Novelty*: Could be visualized as a ghostly outline or subtle gravitational distortion pulling nearby As inward before anyone understands why.

**[Analogy #6]**: Tipping Point / Phase Transition
*Concept*: Like water freezing — nothing visible changes at 1.0°C vs 0.1°C, but at 0°C the whole structure suddenly reorganizes. A→B isn't linear accumulation, it's a phase transition.
*Novelty*: The GIF could show gradual, almost boring clustering then a sudden dramatic reorganization — mirroring the lived experience of years of nothing, then sudden clarity.

**Key Insight — Three Timescales:**
1. **Fast** — U binding/rejection (seconds, instinctive)
2. **Medium** — A frontier growth (new branches forming)
3. **Slow** — A→B maturation (gradual trust-building with sudden phase-transition moment)

### Morphological Analysis

**[Morpho #1]**: Node Style — Solid spheres, uniform surface, differentiated by colour only. Abstract/graph-theory aesthetic.

**[Morpho #2]**: Edges — Slight curves, thickness/opacity encoding connection strength. B-B thick and bright, frontier A-A thin and faint. Always visible.

**[Morpho #3]**: Camera — Fixed angle.

**[Morpho #4]**: Background — Pure black. Nodes and edges glow against void.

**[Morpho #5]**: GIF 1 "Path of the A Frontier" — 5 new As pop in sequentially at the frontier. Network subtly rebalances toward the growth area, like the whole structure leans toward where attention is.

**[Morpho #6]**: GIF 2 "As being turned into Bs" — Colour change happens first (red→green snap), then the new B drifts inward toward the core, gently pulling connected nodes along with it but with weaker gravitational effect. One or two transitions.

**[Morpho #7]**: GIF 3 "Us being offered up" — Two Us approach from outside. One emanates from a small book icon, one from a stick figure. Strong agonist snaps into place as A. Weak agonist wobbles, detaches, drifts slowly away while fading to nothing.

### Ecosystem Thinking

**[Ecosystem #1]**: Shared base state — All three GIFs start from the same initial graph (5 Bs clustered centrally, ~15-20 As in layers radiating outward, varying connection density). Each GIF shows one event type playing out on that same network.

**System Architecture:** One base graph generator + three animation functions. The three GIFs represent complementary processes: growing (energy/expansion), consolidating (stability/structure), and filtering (selectivity/boundary).

## Idea Organization and Prioritization
